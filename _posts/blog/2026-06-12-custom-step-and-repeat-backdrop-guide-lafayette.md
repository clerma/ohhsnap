A sequin wall photographs beautifully, takes ten minutes to set up, and works
for 80% of the events we book in Lafayette. The other 20% need something
sequin can't do: a custom-printed step-and-repeat backdrop with a logo, a
monogram, or an event title repeated edge to edge.

If you're planning a gala, a brand activation, or a wedding where the
backdrop needs to do real branding work, here's how to think about it.

## When a custom step-and-repeat is the right call

Three signs you should commission one instead of pulling from our in-house
collection:

1. **You need to feature a logo (or several).** Sponsor walls at nonprofit
   galas, branded corporate activations, and ribbon-cuttings all need a
   backdrop that puts the logos in every photo a guest takes.
2. **The event has a unique visual identity.** A wedding with a custom
   monogram, a milestone birthday with a specific motif, a launch event
   with a campaign creative — sequin can't carry that.
3. **The photos will be shared publicly.** When event photos hit Instagram,
   LinkedIn, or local press, the brand should be doing the work in every
   frame.

## What we've learned from past Lafayette projects

We've designed and produced custom backdrops for events including:

- **One Acadiana's Regional Celebration** — branded with the
  organization's identity for a room full of regional business leaders.
- **AAF Acadiana's American Advertising Awards (the ADDYs)** — the
  "Order of the Eternal Eye" themed backdrop matched the event's
  creative campaign.
- **Lourdes Foundation Blessings Soirée** — logo-forward design with
  themed speech-bubble props built by our friends at Prop Box.

Three takeaways from those projects:

**Logo repetition isn't random.** A good step-and-repeat uses a measured
pattern — typically 6 to 9 inches between logo instances — so logos appear
clearly behind every guest regardless of height. Too tight and the backdrop
looks busy; too loose and the branding disappears in close-up shots.

**Match the print to the print booth.** Glossy prints reflect studio
lighting badly. We default to matte vinyl with a low-sheen finish so the
logos read cleanly in both portrait booth photos and roaming photographer
shots.

**Plan around the print booth's framing.** Our Portrait Booth shoots with a
fairly tight crop. The logo size and spacing need to be calibrated so at
least one full logo lands behind every guest, even in a single-person shot.

## The production timeline

Custom step-and-repeats are not last-minute work. From the moment you send
us your final approved artwork:

- **Proof and revisions:** 3–5 business days
- **Print production:** 5–7 business days
- **Stand fabrication (if needed):** 5–7 business days

For a smooth Lafayette event, send us artwork 4 weeks before the date. We
can move faster in a pinch, but rush production costs more and leaves no
margin for revisions.

## What we need from you

To quote a custom step-and-repeat we need:

- Vector artwork of the logo(s) — `.ai`, `.svg`, or `.pdf`
- Brand color codes (PMS or hex)
- Backdrop dimensions — most events use 8' × 8' or 8' × 10'
- Event date and venue (so we can plan delivery)

If you don't have vector files, we can usually work from a high-res PNG, but
the print won't be as crisp.
