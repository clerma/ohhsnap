Summer in Acadiana means long receptions, outdoor ceremonies, and guests who
want every excuse to step out of the heat for a minute. That's where a wedding
photo booth earns its keep — it gives the bridal party and the band a break,
keeps guests entertained between dinner and dancing, and sends everyone home
with a printed (or texted) memory.

Here's what couples across Lafayette and the rest of Acadiana are booking with
us this season.

## The Glam Booth keeps stealing the show

Black-and-white "Vanity Fair"–style portraits with subtle skin smoothing have
been our most-requested wedding upgrade for two summers in a row. Couples love
that the prints look like a magazine spread instead of a typical strip.
Bridesmaids love the lighting. Photographers love that it doesn't compete with
their work — it complements it.

If you've ever seen a celebrity-style wedding photo at a reception and
wondered how they pulled it off, that's our **Glam Booth** in action.

## 360 video booths for the dance floor moment

The 360 booth has gone from "fun novelty" to "the highlight reel of the
reception." Couples are scheduling them right after the first dance, when the
floor opens up — guests rotate through the booth, get a cinematic slow-motion
clip with the wedding's music, and post it before they sit down for cake.

For larger Acadiana weddings (250+ guests), we usually pair the 360 booth
with a portrait booth so the lines stay short and everyone gets both
experiences.

## Custom monograms have replaced generic templates

The era of "wedding photo booth strip with a stock heart border" is over.
Every wedding we work in 2026 includes a fully custom print and digital
overlay — names, date, and a design pulled from the couple's invitation
suite or monogram.

Our in-house designer handles the artwork, sends a proof before the wedding,
and revises until it's right. Your printed strip becomes a piece of the
wedding's visual identity, not an afterthought.

## Instant digital sharing isn't optional anymore

Guests at a 2026 Acadiana wedding expect their photos to land in their phone
before they leave the reception. Every Ohh Snap rental — even the print-only
Portrait Booth — includes instant text and AirDrop sharing. The bride's
hashtag fills up before the cake is cut.

The bonus: an online gallery delivered the next day, so couples can re-share
the best shots in the weeks following the wedding without hunting through a
photographer's proof gallery.

## On-site attendant, always

Summer weddings in Louisiana are not the moment to find out your photo booth
is unattended. Heat, humidity, and a long bar line mean someone needs to be
managing props, queueing guests, and making sure prints come out clean.
Every Ohh Snap wedding rental in Lafayette includes a trained attendant —
no exceptions.
