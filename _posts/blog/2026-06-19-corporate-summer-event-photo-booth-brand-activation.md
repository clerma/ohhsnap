Summer is corporate event season in Acadiana. Crawfish boils run into
employee appreciation cookouts, mid-year sales kickoffs replace last year's
"we'll do it next quarter" plans, and Q3 conference and ribbon-cutting
schedules get locked. Every one of those events is a chance to make
something shareable — not just memorable.

Here's where an Ohh Snap photo booth pulls its weight at a Lafayette
corporate event.

## The booth as a content engine

The shift over the last few years has been pretty clear. Companies aren't
booking photo booths because they're "fun" — they're booking them because
the booth produces share-ready content with the company's branding on it,
in real time, that employees and customers will actually post.

Every photo we deliver — print, GIF, boomerang, 360 video — carries the
event's custom overlay. When a guest texts that photo to themselves and
posts to Instagram or LinkedIn, your brand is the first thing anyone sees.

For Lafayette companies, that's a more authentic reach than a paid social
ad, and it costs less than a single ad campaign.

## Three event formats where the booth shines

We see the same playbook work over and over in Lafayette:

**Employee appreciation events.** A custom backdrop with the company logo
plus a stack of branded props. Employees take photos with their team, send
them around the office (and on LinkedIn), and HR gets a quiet boost to its
employer-brand metric. We've done this for organizations including ULL,
Aflac, Our Lady of Lourdes, and the City Club at River Ranch.

**Sales kickoffs and mid-year meetings.** Internal events get a bad rap as
boring, but a photo booth at the cocktail hour gives attendees something to
do besides scroll. Bonus: the GIFs and boomerangs become hype-reel content
for the company's internal comms team to use for the next 12 months.

**Ribbon-cuttings and grand openings.** Branded backdrop + roaming
photographer is the combo. The roaming photographer captures the ceremony
itself; the booth runs after the cut for guest photos. Photos hit social
the same hour.

## The Acadiana flavor: outdoor activations

Lafayette summer events skew outdoor more than the national average — crawfish
boils, brewery cookouts, festival sponsorships. Two things to plan around:

**Heat and shade.** Most of our booth setups need shade. We bring our own
canopy on outdoor bookings, but the venue still needs a flat surface with
electrical access within 50 feet.

**Humidity and prints.** Standard 4×6 prints handle Louisiana humidity well,
but we recommend an on-site print envelope (sleeves) for outdoor events so
the photos travel home in good shape.
